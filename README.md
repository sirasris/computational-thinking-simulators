# computational-thinking-simulators
Various simulators of life science systems for teaching computational thinking

## Scenario 1: Finding a good cutoff for a diagnostic test
You are developing a new diagnostic test by comparing biomarker expression levels from 100 control and 100 patients. The goal is to achieve at least 80% PPV while maximizing sensitivity.

#### Your task is to identify a good biomarker expression cutoff and to explore its relationship with the disease prevalence

## Scenario 2: Hospital performance paradox
Can a hospital provide better services for all patient subgroups but still achieve an overall lower treatment success rate compare to another hospital? This is called a Simpson's Paradox.

#### Your task is to explore the settings where Simpson's Paradox occur and explain this phenomenon

## Scenario 3: Safety in number
Imagine that a batch of medical records is leaked and your files is in it. The records were indexed by birthdate. So, it would be lucky for you if there are other people with the same birthdate in the leaked batch. What is the probability that such incident will occur?

#### Your task is to figure out the minimal number of leaked records that ensure certain probability of two patients sharing the same birthdate
